Bab Al Bahrain

Overview:

Bab Al Bahrain, meaning "Gateway of Bahrain", is an iconic historical building located at the entrance of the Manama Souq. It once overlooked the sea and now stands as a symbol of Bahrain’s trading and cultural past.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 1945
  • Main architectural feature: Arched facade with Islamic motifs
  • Used for: Government offices, tourist hub, souq gateway

Location & Access:

Located at Government Avenue, Manama. Easy access by car, bus, or on foot. Parking is available nearby.

Historical Background:

Designed by British adviser Charles Belgrave, Bab Al Bahrain was the former administrative center of Bahrain. The sea was once just behind it before land reclamation projects moved the coastline.

Fun Facts:

  • Now houses a tourist center and cafés.
  • Beautifully lit at night, often decorated during national events.
  • A popular spot for both tourists and photographers.

Visitor Tips:

  • Start your Manama Souq journey here.
  • Visit at sunset for a lively local atmosphere.
  • Try traditional Bahraini sweets at nearby shops.

Bab Al Bahrain stands as a bridge between Bahrain’s historic past and its vibrant modern culture.
