Al Fateh Grand Mosque

Overview:

Al Fateh Grand Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and the largest in Bahrain. It is a symbol of Islamic architecture and culture, welcoming worshippers and tourists alike. The mosque is named after Ahmed Al-Fateh, the founder of Bahrain.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 1988
  • Main architectural feature: Giant fiberglass dome, marble floors, Kufic calligraphy
  • Used for: Daily prayers, Friday sermons, religious education, guided tours

Location & Access:

Situated in Juffair, Manama, the mosque is easily reachable by car or public transport. It offers free guided tours for non-Muslims and has a library and information center on-site.

Historical Background:

Built by the late Sheikh Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa, the mosque was constructed using materials from all over the world — Italian marble, Indian teakwood, and Austrian chandeliers. It reflects Bahrain's openness and rich Islamic heritage.

Fun Facts:

  • The dome is the largest fiberglass dome in the world.
  • The mosque can accommodate over 7,000 worshippers.
  • Tour guides offer explanations in multiple languages, including English, French, and German.

Visitor Tips:

  • Open for tours from 9 AM to 4 PM except Fridays.
  • Modest attire required; abayas and headscarves provided for women.
  • Don’t miss the intricate Arabic calligraphy and peaceful atmosphere.

Visiting during Ramadan offers a unique spiritual experience and traditional hospitality.
