Abdullah Al Zayed House for Bahraini Press Heritage

Overview:

This restored heritage house honors Abdullah Al Zayed, the founder of Bahrainâ€™s first newspaper, and showcases the evolution of journalism and literature in the Kingdom.

Highlights:

  • Established in: early 20th century
  • Main architectural feature: Traditional coral stone Bahraini house
  • Used for: Museum, cultural archive, educational tours

Location & Access:

Located in Muharraq, the house is part of the Pearling Path and can be reached by foot or taxi.

Historical Background:

The house belonged to Abdullah Al Zayed, a prominent intellectual and media figure. It was restored by the Bahrain Authority for Culture and Antiquities as part of the national heritage revival.

Fun Facts:

  • Features early printing equipment and vintage photographs.
  • Offers a library with rare Bahraini publications.
  • Often included in heritage walking tours of Muharraq.

Visitor Tips:

  • Guided tours are available on request.
  • Combine with visits to other Pearl Path landmarks.
  • Photography is allowed inside and outside.
