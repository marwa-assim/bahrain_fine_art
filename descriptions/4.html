Bahrain World Trade Center

Overview:

The Bahrain World Trade Center (BWTC) is an architectural marvel and the first skyscraper in the world to integrate wind turbines into its design. It stands as a symbol of Bahrain’s modernity and sustainability efforts, prominently located in Manama.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 2008
  • Main architectural feature: Twin towers with three integrated wind turbines
  • Used for: Commercial offices, retail, luxury hospitality

Location & Access:

BWTC is located along the King Faisal Highway in Manama, easily accessible from all major parts of the city. The Moda Mall at its base offers a range of luxury shopping and dining options.

Historical Background:

Designed by the global architectural firm Atkins, the BWTC pioneered sustainable architecture in the Middle East. It stands 240 meters tall and consists of 50 floors, drawing inspiration from traditional Arabian sails.

Fun Facts:

  • First skyscraper in the world with integrated wind turbines producing renewable energy.
  • Received multiple international awards for design and sustainability.
  • The turbines can generate up to 15% of the towers’ energy needs under optimal conditions.

Visitor Tips:

  • Open to visitors through the attached Moda Mall and restaurants.
  • Guided architectural tours available upon request for groups.
  • Great photo opportunity from the Corniche area at sunset.

Visit the BWTC in the evening when it’s beautifully illuminated against the Bahrain skyline.
