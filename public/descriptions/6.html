Al Fateh Grand Mosque

Overview:

The Al Fateh Grand Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world and the largest place of worship in Bahrain. It is a prominent Islamic landmark, welcoming visitors of all faiths to learn about Islam and Bahraini culture.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 1988
  • Main architectural feature: Large central dome made of fiberglass, Kufic calligraphy, marble floors
  • Used for: Worship, educational tours, religious events

Location & Access:

Located in Juffair, Manama, the mosque is easily accessible by car or public transport. There is ample parking and a visitor center available on site.

Historical Background:

Named after Ahmed Al-Fateh, the founder of modern Bahrain, the mosque was built under the patronage of the late Emir Sheikh Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa. It accommodates over 7,000 worshippers and is also home to the National Library of Bahrain.

Fun Facts:

  • The dome is one of the largest fiberglass domes in the world.
  • Open to tourists with multilingual guides offering free tours.
  • Features intricate Islamic decorations and Bahraini craftsmanship.

Visitor Tips:

  • Open Saturday to Thursday, 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM
  • Modest clothing is required; abayas provided at entrance for women
  • Photography is allowed in most areas

Attend the guided tour for an enriching cultural and spiritual experience.
