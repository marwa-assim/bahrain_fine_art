Al Jasra Handicrafts Center

Overview:

Al Jasra Handicrafts Center is a cultural village that preserves Bahrain’s traditional arts and crafts, supporting local artisans and offering visitors a glimpse into authentic heritage.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 1990s
  • Main architectural feature: Traditional Bahraini courtyard buildings
  • Used for: Craft workshops, exhibitions, cultural education

Location & Access:

Located in Al Jasra village on the western coast, the center is reachable by car and situated near the King Fahd Causeway.

Historical Background:

The center was developed as part of Bahrain’s cultural preservation efforts and showcases crafts like pottery, basket weaving, woodworking, and fabric making.

Fun Facts:

  • Each artisan maintains a workshop that visitors can enter.
  • It is near the birthplace of the late Emir Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa.
  • Handmade souvenirs available for purchase.

Visitor Tips:

  • Best visited during daylight hours.
  • Support artisans by buying their creations directly.
  • Ask questions — craftsmen are happy to share their stories.

Experience the soul of Bahraini culture through hands-on crafts and warm hospitality.
