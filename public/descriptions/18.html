Al-Khamis Mosque

Overview:

Al-Khamis Mosque is one of the oldest mosques in Bahrain and the entire Arabian Peninsula. It reflects the early spread of Islam in the region and is an important symbol of Islamic architectural heritage.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 7th–8th century CE
  • Main architectural feature: Dual minarets, stone and coral architecture
  • Used for: Worship, Islamic education, and gatherings

Location & Access:

Located in Khamis area on Shaikh Salman Highway. The site is fenced with clear signs, and access is free to the public.

Historical Background:

Believed to have been built during the Umayyad period, it was expanded and renovated several times over the centuries. Its ruins remain a testament to early Islamic life in Bahrain.

Fun Facts:

  • The twin minarets were added in the 15th century.
  • The site includes remnants of early Islamic inscriptions.
  • Often featured in Islamic history documentaries.

Visitor Tips:

  • Best visited in the morning for good lighting and fewer crowds.
  • Modest dress is advised as it’s a religious site.
  • No entrance fee — photography is allowed.

A must-visit for those interested in the roots of Islamic culture in the Gulf.
