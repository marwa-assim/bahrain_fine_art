Shaikh Isa bin Salman Causeway

Overview:

The Shaikh Isa bin Salman Causeway is one of Bahrain’s most vital infrastructure links, connecting Muharraq Island to the capital Manama. It is named after the late Amir of Bahrain, Sheikh Isa bin Salman Al Khalifa.

Highlights:

  • Opened in: 1997
  • Main feature: Dual carriageway bridge with modern lighting and views
  • Used for: Daily commute, connecting airport and city

Location & Access:

Strategically located between Bahrain International Airport and the capital, it offers scenic views of the coastline. Open to all vehicle types.

Historical Background:

The causeway was built to facilitate rapid development and connectivity, and honors the legacy of a key figure in Bahrain’s modernization era.

Fun Facts:

  • Equipped with smart lighting systems for night navigation.
  • Frequently featured in aerial shots of Bahrain.
  • Supports both tourism and trade by easing logistics.

Visitor Tips:

  • Enjoy the view during sunrise or sunset drives.
  • Stop nearby at cafes overlooking the water.
  • Photography is best from nearby bridges or elevated spots.

Drive through to experience Bahrain’s blend of coastal charm and urban infrastructure.
