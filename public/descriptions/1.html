Barbar Temple

Overview:

The Barbar Temple is one of Bahrain’s most ancient archaeological sites, part of the Dilmun civilization. It is believed to be a sacred temple dedicated to the Sumerian god Enki, featuring altars, spring-fed wells, and limestone platforms.

Highlights:

  • Established in: around 3000 BC
  • Main architectural feature: Limestone platforms, underground spring shrine
  • Used for: Worship, ritual ceremonies, offerings

Location & Access:

Located in Barbar village, northwest of Manama, easily accessible by car or taxi. Nearby visitor signs and historical plaques explain the site’s cultural context.

Historical Background:

Discovered by Danish archaeologists in 1954, the site consists of three temples built over centuries. The complex showcases the Dilmun era’s engineering and spiritual life and includes a freshwater spring, symbolizing the abzu (underworld waters).

Fun Facts:

  • Rediscovered in the 1950s with excavation led by Peter Glob.
  • The temple includes a natural spring once considered sacred.
  • Many artifacts from the site are displayed at Bahrain National Museum.

Visitor Tips:

  • Best visited in the morning or evening to avoid heat.
  • Wear comfortable shoes — the ground is uneven and sandy.
  • Photography allowed; bring water and sunscreen.
