Wind Tower (Barjeel)

Overview:

The Wind Tower, or Barjeel, is a traditional architectural feature used in Bahrain and the Gulf to cool buildings naturally. These towers are part of Bahrain's vernacular heritage, showcasing ingenious passive design solutions suited to the hot climate.

Highlights:

  • Established in: 19th–20th century
  • Main architectural feature: Four-sided open-topped towers that funnel cool air inside
  • Used for: Ventilation and passive cooling in homes and buildings

Location & Access:

Wind Towers can be found in older neighborhoods like Muharraq. Some restored examples are part of heritage trails and open to public viewing.

Historical Background:

Barjeel structures were adopted across the Arabian Peninsula for their cooling efficiency. They are an integral part of Bahrain’s pre-electric architectural solutions, often found in merchant houses.

Fun Facts:

  • Also known as "badgir" in Persian architecture.
  • Some towers are now integrated into museums and cultural centers.
  • They inspired modern eco-design strategies in the Gulf region.

Visitor Tips:

  • Join heritage tours to learn about sustainable architecture.
  • Photograph during golden hour for dramatic shadows and light.
  • Look for interactive exhibits in cultural houses with restored towers.

Visiting a wind tower is a great way to explore Bahrain’s practical creativity before air-conditioning existed.
